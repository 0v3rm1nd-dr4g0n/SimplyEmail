DIACAP/DoD RMF

The transition from the DIACAP to the Risk Management Framework (RMF) for the Department of Defense (DoD) enables agencies to effectively and efficiently manage the life cycle cybersecurity risk to information technology. The process establishes baseline information assurance controls to secure information systems, and provide enhanced visibility to senior leadership, resulting in more informed, risk-based decisions.

The RMF represents a transformation in the way the DoD conducts business, and aligns organizations with standardized Federal government risk management policies and procedures. The transformation brings a new lexicon, and approach to system categorization, assessment, and monitoring. Veris Group addresses these challenges with our RMF practitioners who helped shape the current policy, and have successfully transitioned organizations in the public sector to the new framework.

Veris Group has many years of experience providing customized DIACAP and RMF solutions to the DoD and other federal agencies. Our RMF approach builds upon the 6 Steps of the RMF (Categorize, Select, Implement, Assess, Authorize, Monitor) and incorporates our extensive knowledge in delivering RMF services to the federal civilian sector. This approach enables us to deliver efficient and effective transformation from DIACAP processes for our clients.

All DoD organizations are required to migrate to the RMF for DoD based on the criteria set forth in DoD Instruction 8510.01, Enclosure 8 (graphic – see below).

The transformation from DIACAP to DoD RMF presents multiple challenges for organizations, including:

  • Changing traditional Certification & Accreditation (C&A) processes, i.e., adopting NIST Special Publication 800-37 RMF processes
  • Shifting of roles and responsibilities
  • Implementing new cybersecurity controls based on CNSS 1253
  • Adopting new terminology for cybersecurity roles and processes

To address these challenges, our team:

  • Performs a gap analysis of existing processes and technologies
  • Identifies the scope of work required to fully implement DoD RMF requirements
  • Leverages current documentation and procedures where possible
  • Develops and implements risk focused tools and procedures
  • Delivers a compliant cybersecurity program focused on risk-based decision making

We capitalize on our experience in Continuous Diagnostics and Mitigation (CDM), continuous assessment, and cybersecurity integration with the System Development Life Cycle (SDLC) to implement a complete cybersecurity program that delivers cyber risk management, not just C&A packages.

Further, we integrate the Project Management Institute (PMI) and Capability Maturity Model Integration (CMMI) Maturity Level 2 methodologies on all DoD RMF efforts to ensure consistency, repeatability, quality, and efficiency.

Veris Group’s DIACAP/DoD RMF services help:

  • Empower your risk-based decision making based on impact to the organizational mission.
  • Reduce time spent obtaining DoD and other federal agency and department authorizations by providing reciprocal acceptance.
  • Increase the likelihood of your projects being executed on-time and on-budget by building security into systems, instead of bolting on afterwards.
  • Increase efficiency through information assurance control inheritance and re-use.

Veris Group’s DIACAP/DoD RMF services:

  • Tailor the RMF to each organization and align supporting functions to realize the efficiencies of the framework.
  • Integrate with System Development Life Cycle (SDLC) and acquisition system activities to help ensure a cost-effective transition from the onset.
  • Provide continuous monitoring for near real-time decisions.
  • Leverage the concepts of inheritance and similarities in control implementation to consolidate systems into logical boundaries.
  • Produce fewer C&A packages and reduce the amount of resources needed to complete the overall process.
  • Incorporate our RMF expertise to help organizations transition seamlessly from DITSCAP to DIACAP to RMF across multiple agencies.
